Privacy
Policy.

Last updated: April 2025

Traced is a free, open-source email tracking tool. We believe in full transparency. This policy explains exactly what data we collect, why, and how it's used.

What data we collect

When a recipient opens a tracked email or clicks a tracked link, Traced logs:

  • Timestamp of the open or click
  • Approximate IP address (for geolocation only — not stored precisely)
  • User-agent string (to detect device type and email client)
  • Whether the email was opened (top pixel) or fully read (bottom pixel)

We do not collect the content of your emails. We do not store email bodies, attachments, or personal message content.

Who can see this data

Only the sender of the tracked email can see tracking data. The dashboard at traced.mikaelsyed.me/dashboard shows your own tracked emails only.

Recipients of tracked emails are not notified that tracking is active. This is standard practice across all email tracking tools.

Data retention

In the default self-hosted configuration, tracking data is stored in-memory and cleared when the server restarts. If you configure persistent storage (e.g. Supabase), data is retained until you delete it.

Third parties

Traced does not share any data with third parties. The server code is fully open source at github.com/exotrezy/traced-server.

Self-hosting

If you self-host Traced on your own infrastructure, you are the data controller. This privacy policy applies to the hosted instance at traced.mikaelsyed.me only.
